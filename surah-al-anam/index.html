Surah Al-An’am (Chapter 6: The Cattle) - Verses 1-165 Analysis 📖

📜 Introduction: Setting the Scene

  • Place and Context of Revelation

    • Historical Setting: Revealed primarily in Mecca (circa 610-622 CE), during the later Meccan period before the Hijra, when Muslims faced intense persecution from the Quraysh. Some scholars suggest a few verses (e.g., 20, 23, 91) may have Medinan elements.
    • Geographical Context: Mecca, a polytheistic hub centered around the Ka’bah, where idol worship dominated, and monotheism was challenged.
    • Circumstances: Surah Al-An’am focuses on affirming Tawhid (monotheism), refuting idolatry, and clarifying prophethood. It addresses the Quraysh’s objections, emphasizes God’s signs in creation, and provides moral guidance, serving as a theological foundation for the early Muslim community.

  • 🔑 Key Themes and Topics

    • ☝️ Tawhid (Oneness of God): Central emphasis on God’s unity, power, and creation, rejecting polytheism.
    • 🌍 Signs in Creation: Highlights nature and human existence as evidence of God’s existence and mercy.
    • 🧭 Prophethood and Revelation: Defends the Prophet Muhammad’s mission and links it to previous prophets.
    • ⚖️ Moral Accountability: Stresses individual responsibility, warning against disbelief and idolatry.

  • 🌍 Relevance Today

    • ⏳ Universal Monotheism: Offers a timeless call to recognize one God, relevant in pluralistic and secular societies.
    • 🤝 Interfaith Connections: Affirms shared prophetic heritage with Jews and Christians, fostering dialogue.
    • 💡 Ethical Reflection: Encourages contemplation of nature and personal purpose, applicable to environmental and spiritual discussions.

6:139وَقَالُوا مَا فِي بُطُونِ هَٰذِهِ الْأَنْعَامِ...Unhone kaha, “Jo in maweshiyon ke pet mein hai...”They say, “What is in the wombs of these cattle...”🚫 False Rules: Like Jewish ritual critiques (Isaiah 1:11).
6:140قَدْ خَسِرَ الَّذِينَ قَتَلُوا أَوْلَادَهُمْ سَفَهًا...Haar gaye jinhone apne aulaad ko jahalat mein qatl kiya...Those who killed their children in folly have lost...😔 Tragic Loss: Like Hindu non-violence (Ahimsa).
6:141وَهُوَ الَّذِي أَنْشَأَ جَنَّاتٍ مَعْرُوشَاتٍ...Usne baghaat aur angoor ke baagh paida kiye...It is He Who produced gardens, trellised and untrellised...🌳 God’s Provision: Like Christian provider God (Matthew 6:26).
6:142وَمِنَ الْأَنْعَامِ حَمُولَةً وَفَرْشًا...Aur maweshiyon mein bojh uthane wale aur farsh ke...And of the cattle, some for burden and some for meat...🐄 Livestock Blessing: Like Jewish agricultural blessings (Deuteronomy 28:4).
6:143ثَمَانِيَةَ أَزْوَاجٍ مِنَ الضَّأْنِ اثْنَيْنِ...Aath jode, bhedon ke do, bakriyon ke do...Eight pairs: two of sheep, two of goats...🐑 Cattle Pairs: Contextual; like Jewish clean animals (Leviticus 11).
6:144وَمِنَ الْإِبِلِ اثْنَيْنِ وَمِنَ الْبَقَرِ اثْنَيْنِ...Oonton ke do, gaiyon ke do...Two of camels, two of cows...🐫 Livestock Rules: Like Hindu reverence for cows, but contextual.
6:145قُلْ لَا أَجِدُ فِي مَا أُوحِيَ إِلَيَّ مُحَرَّمًا...Kaho, “Mujhe wahi mein koi cheez haram nahi milti...”Say, “I find not in what was revealed to me anything forbidden...”🍽️ Dietary Clarity: Like Christian food freedom (Acts 10:15).
6:146وَعَلَى الَّذِينَ هَادُوا حَرَّمْنَا كُلَّ ذِي ظُفُرٍ...Yahudiyon par humne har naakhun wala janwar haram kiya...For those who are Jews, We forbade every animal with undivided hooves...🚫 Jewish Dietary Laws: Matches Leviticus 11:3-7.
6:147فَإِنْ كَذَّبُوكَ فَقُلْ رَبُّكُمْ ذُو رَحْمَةٍ وَاسِعَةٍ...Agar woh tujhe jhuthlaayein toh kaho, “Tumhara Rab rehmat ka malik hai...”If they deny you, say, “Your Lord is full of mercy...”🕊️ Mercy and Justice: Like Christian grace and judgment (Romans 11:22).
6:148سَيَقُولُ الَّذِينَ أَشْرَكُوا لَوْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ...Mushrik kahenge, “Agar Allah chahta toh...”The idolaters will say, “If Allah had willed...”🚫 False Excuses: Like Christian blaming God (James 1:13).
6:149قُلْ فَلِلَّهِ الْحُجَّةُ الْبَالِغَةُ...Kaho, “Allah ke paas mukammal hujjat hai...”Say, “To Allah belongs the conclusive argument...”📜 God’s Proof: Like Jewish divine evidence (Psalm 19:7).
6:150قُلْ هَاتُوا شُهَدَاءَكُمُ الَّذِينَ يَشْهَدُونَ...Kaho, “Apne gawah lao jo gawahi dein...”Say, “Bring your witnesses who testify...”⚖️ Demand Evidence: Like Christian testing claims (1 Thessalonians 5:21).
6:151قُلْ تَعَالَوْا أَتْلُ مَا حَرَّمَ رَبُّكُمْ عَلَيْكُمْ...Kaho, “Aao, main padh kar sunayun jo tumhare Rab ne haram kiya...”Say, “Come, I will recite what your Lord has prohibited...”🚫 Moral Laws: Like Jewish Ten Commandments (Exodus 20).
6:152وَلَا تَقْرَبُوا مَالَ الْيَتِيمِ إِلَّا بِالَّتِي هِيَ أَحْسَنُ...Yateem ke maal ke paas na jao siwaye behtar tareeke ke...Do not approach the orphan’s property except in the best way...⚖️ Protect Orphans: Like Jewish care for vulnerable (Deuteronomy 24:17).
6:153وَأَنَّ هَٰذَا صِرَاطِي مُسْتَقِيمًا...Yeh mera seedha raasta hai...This is My straight path...🛤️ Right Path: Like Christian narrow way (Matthew 7:14).
6:154ثُمَّ آتَيْنَا مُوسَى الْكِتَابَ تَمَامًا...Phir humne Moosa ko Kitaab di...Then We gave Moses the Book...📜 Torah’s Completion: Like Jewish law’s perfection (Psalm 19:7).
6:155

وَهَٰذَا كِتَابٌ أَنْزَلْنَاهُ مُبَارَكٌ فَاتَّبِعُوهُ...

Yeh ek kitaab hai jo humne utari, mubarak hai, iski pairvi karo...

This is a blessed Book We have revealed, so follow it...

📖 Blessed Quran: Like Jewish Torah’s blessing (Psalm 119:1). Encourages adherence to divine guidance.

6:156أَنْ تَقُولُوا إِنَّمَا أُنْزِلَ الْكِتَابُ عَلَىٰ طَائِفَتَيْنِ مِنْ قَبْلِنَا...Taki tum na kaho ke kitaab sirf hum se pehle do girohon par utari gayi...Lest you say, “The Book was only revealed to two groups before us...”📜 Universal Scripture: Like Christian gospel for all nations (Matthew 28:19). Refutes exclusivity of prior revelations.
6:157أَوْ تَقُولُوا لَوْ أَنَّا أُنْزِلَ عَلَيْنَا الْكِتَابُ لَكُنَّا أَهْدَىٰ...Ya kaho, “Agar hum par kitaab utari hoti toh hum zyada hidayat yafta hote...”Or say, “If only the Book had been revealed to us, we would have been more guided...”🚫 No Excuses: Like Christian accountability with truth (John 15:22). Removes pretexts for disbelief.
6:158هَلْ يَنْظُرُونَ إِلَّا أَنْ تَأْتِيَهُمُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ أَوْ يَأْتِيَ رَبُّكَ...Kya woh intezar karte hain ke farishte aayein ya tera Rab aaye...Do they wait for the angels to come or your Lord to come...⚖️ Day of Judgment: Like Christian sudden return of Christ (Matthew 24:36). Warns against delaying repentance.
6:159إِنَّ الَّذِينَ فَرَّقُوا دِينَهُمْ وَكَانُوا شِيَعًا...Jo apne deen ko tukdon mein baant dete hain aur girohon mein bat jate hain...Those who divide their religion and become sects...🚫 Avoid Division: Like Christian call for unity (1 Corinthians 1:10). Condemns sectarianism in faith.
6:160مَنْ جَاءَ بِالْحَسَنَةِ فَلَهُ عَشْرُ أَمْثَالِهَا...Jo nek kaam laaye, uske liye uska das guna hai...Whoever brings a good deed will have ten times its like...🌟 Multiplied Reward: Like Jewish reward for righteousness (Isaiah 3:10). Emphasizes divine generosity for good deeds.
6:161قُلْ إِنَّنِي هَدَانِي رَبِّي إِلَىٰ صِرَاطٍ مُسْتَقِيمٍ...Kaho, “Mere Rab ne mujhe seedha raasta dikhaya...”Say, “My Lord has guided me to a straight path...”🛤️ Straight Path: Like Christian narrow way (Matthew 7:14). Affirms the Prophet’s guidance to true monotheism.
6:162قُلْ إِنَّ صَلَاتِي وَنُسُكِي وَمَحْيَايَ وَمَمَاتِي لِلَّهِ...Kaho, “Meri namaaz, meri qurbani, mera jeena aur marna Allah ke liye hai...”Say, “My prayer, my sacrifice, my life, and my death are for Allah...”🙏 Total Devotion: Like Hindu complete surrender (Bhagavad Gita 9:27). Dedicates all actions to God.
6:163لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ وَبِذَٰلِكَ أُمِرْتُ...Uska koi shareek nahi, aur mujhe isi ka hukm diya gaya...He has no partner, and this I have been commanded...☝️ Pure Monotheism: Like Jewish Shema (Deuteronomy 6:4). Reinforces exclusive worship of God.
6:164قُلْ أَغَيْرَ اللَّهِ أَبْتَغِي رَبًّا وَهُوَ رَبُّ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ...Kaho, “Kya main Allah ke siwa koi Rab talash karun, jabki woh har cheez ka Rab hai...”Say, “Shall I seek a lord other than Allah, when He is the Lord of all things...”🌍 God’s Lordship: Like Christian “one Lord” (Ephesians 4:5). Emphasizes God’s universal sovereignty.
6:165وَهُوَ الَّذِي جَعَلَكُمْ خَلَائِفَ الْأَرْضِ وَرَفَعَ بَعْضَكُمْ...Wohi hai jisne tumhein zameen ka khalifa banaya aur tum mein se kuchh ko darje diya...It is He Who made you successors on the earth and raised some in ranks...⚖️ Human Responsibility: Like Jewish stewardship of earth (Genesis 1:28). Highlights accountability as God’s vicegerents.
